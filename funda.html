CYBERSECURITY FUNDAMENTALS

FINEX outlines the essential fundamentals of cybersecurity, emphasizing the importance of securing personal data and implementing preventive measures to protect against cyber threats.

1 Password Management

Using strong, unique passwords for each account to protect against unauthorized access.

2 Encryption

Converting sensitive data into unreadable text to ensure confidentiality when transmitted over the internet.

3 Firewalls

Hardware or software that monitors and controls incoming and outgoing network traffic to prevent unauthorized access.

4 Antivirus Protection

Software that detects, prevents, and removes malicious programs like viruses, worms, and trojans from your system.

5 Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

An extra layer of security that requires both a password and a second form of verification to access accounts.

6 Data Backups

Regularly backing up critical data to prevent loss due to system failures, cyberattacks, or natural disasters.

7 Security Patches

Keeping software up to date by applying patches and updates that fix vulnerabilities and enhance security.

8 User Access Control

Implementing the principle of least privilege, ensuring users only have access to the information they need.

9 Intrusion Detection Systems (IDS)

Systems that monitor network or system activities for malicious activity or policy violations.

10 Phishing Awareness

Educating users on how to identify phishing emails and avoid providing sensitive information to fraudsters.

11 Network Segmentation

Dividing a network into multiple segments to limit the spread of cyberattacks within a network.

12 Secure Communication Channels

Using secure methods like HTTPS or encrypted email to protect communication from eavesdropping.

13 Endpoint Protection

Securing devices such as computers, smartphones, and tablets to prevent them from becoming entry points for cybercriminals.

14 Security Audits

Regularly reviewing and assessing your network, systems, and policies to identify vulnerabilities and areas for improvement.

15 Risk Management

Identifying, assessing, and mitigating potential security risks to prevent data breaches and other cybersecurity threats.

16 Incident Response Planning

Developing a clear plan for responding to cyberattacks or security incidents to minimize damage and recovery time.

17 Secure Software Development

Writing software with built-in security features to reduce vulnerabilities and protect users.

18 Data Masking

Obfuscating sensitive data to prevent unauthorized users from seeing it, especially in non-production environments.

19 Security Training

Regularly educating employees or users on best cybersecurity practices and potential threats to stay vigilant.

20 Compliance with Regulations

Adhering to industry-specific standards and laws, such as GDPR or HIPAA, to ensure legal and secure handling of data.
