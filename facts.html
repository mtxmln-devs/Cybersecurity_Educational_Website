CYBERSECURITY FACTS

FINEX reveals essential cybersecurity truths to help you stay safe online.

1. The First Computer Virus

The first computer virus, "Creeper," appeared in the early 1970s. It displayed the message "I'M THE CREEPER: CATCH ME IF YOU CAN!"

2. Phishing Attacks

Phishing attacks account for a significant portion of cyberattacks. They rely on deception to trick users into revealing sensitive information.

3. Strong Passwords

A strong password is your first line of defense. It should be long, complex, and unique to each account.

4. Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)

Multi-factor authentication (MFA) adds an extra layer of security. It requires multiple forms of verification, like a password and a code from your phone.

5. Ransomware

Ransomware encrypts your files and demands payment for their release. It can cripple businesses and individuals.

6. Cybercriminals Target Everyone

Cybercriminals target both individuals and organizations. No one is immune to cyber threats.

7. Software Updates

Software updates often include critical security patches. Keeping your software up-to-date is essential.

8. Public Wi-Fi Risks

Public Wi-Fi networks can be risky. Avoid accessing sensitive information on unsecured networks.

9. Social Engineering

Social engineering manipulates people into giving up confidential information. Be wary of suspicious requests.

10. Firewalls

Firewalls act as barriers between your network and external threats. They monitor and control network traffic.

11. Encryption

Encryption scrambles data to make it unreadable without a decryption key. It protects sensitive information during transmission and storage.

12. Cybersecurity Awareness

Cybersecurity is not just about technology; it's also about human behavior. User awareness is crucial.

13. Data Breaches

Data breaches can lead to identity theft and financial losses. Protect your personal information.

14. Internet of Things (IoT) Security

The "Internet of Things" (IoT) introduces new security challenges. Connected devices can be vulnerable to cyberattacks.

15. Evolving Cyber Threats

Cybercriminals are constantly evolving their tactics. Cybersecurity requires continuous vigilance.

16. Data Backups

Regularly backing up your data can help you recover from a cyberattack. Store backups in a secure location.

17. Cybersecurity Laws

Cybersecurity laws and regulations are designed to protect individuals and organizations. Compliance is essential.

18. The Dark Web

The dark web is a hidden part of the internet often used for illegal activities. It's important to be aware of its risks.

19. Cybersecurity Professionals

Cybersecurity professionals play a vital role in protecting digital assets. They work to prevent and mitigate cyber threats.

20. Online Safety

Even seemingly harmless online actions can have security implications. Think before you click, share, or download.
