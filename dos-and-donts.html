CYBERSECURITY DO'S AND DONT'S

FINEX shares simple yet crucial dos and don'ts that can significantly enhance your cybersecurity and protect your digital life.

CYBERSECURITY DO'S

Do use strong and unique passwords for each of your online accounts to prevent unauthorized access.
Do enable two-factor authentication wherever possible to add an extra layer of security to your accounts.
Do update your software regularly to patch security vulnerabilities and protect against potential threats.
Do back up your important files frequently to avoid data loss in case of a cyberattack.
Do use a reputable antivirus program to detect and prevent malware from infecting your system.
Do monitor your bank and credit card statements regularly for unusual or unauthorized transactions.
Do encrypt sensitive data before transmitting it online to protect it from being intercepted.
Do secure your Wi-Fi network with a strong password and WPA3 encryption to prevent unauthorized access.
Do educate yourself and others about phishing schemes and how to recognize suspicious emails or messages.
Do log out of your accounts when you're done using them, especially on public or shared devices.

CYBERSECURITY DONT'S

Don't use the same password for multiple accounts, as it increases the risk if one account is compromised.
Don't share your passwords with others, and avoid writing them down in insecure places.
Don't click on suspicious links or attachments in emails, as they may lead to phishing sites or install malware.
Don't ignore security updates or software patches, as they are essential for fixing known vulnerabilities.
Don't use public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities such as banking or logging into important accounts without a VPN.
Don't store passwords in your browser without using a password manager, as they can be easily accessed if your browser is compromised.
Don't leave your devices unattended in public places, as they may be targeted for theft or unauthorized access.
Don't connect to unknown or untrusted networks to avoid exposure to potential cyber threats.
Don't open unsolicited attachments or links in emails from unknown sources, as they could be a trap.
Don't disable your firewall or antivirus protection, as they are your first line of defense against threats.
