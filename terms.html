CYBERSECURITY TERMINOLOGIES

FINEX provides an understanding of key cybersecurity terms that help you navigate and protect your digital environment.

1. Phishing

Phishing is a cyberattack where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations or individuals to steal sensitive information, often through fraudulent emails or websites.

2. Ransomware

Ransomware is a type of malicious software that encrypts a victim's data and demands payment for its decryption, often causing significant disruption to businesses and individuals.

3. Malware

Malware refers to any malicious software, including viruses, worms, and trojans, designed to harm, exploit, or otherwise compromise a computer system's functionality and security.

4. Firewall

A firewall is a network security system designed to monitor and control incoming and outgoing network traffic, establishing a barrier between trusted internal networks and untrusted external networks.

5. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring users to provide two forms of identification, typically a password and a unique code sent to their phone.

6. Encryption

Encryption is the process of converting data into a coded format to prevent unauthorized access, ensuring that only authorized parties can read the information.

7. Spyware

Spyware is malicious software designed to secretly monitor and collect information about a user’s activities, often without their knowledge or consent.

8. Zero-Day Attack

A zero-day attack targets a previously unknown vulnerability in software or hardware, taking advantage of security flaws before they are patched by the manufacturer.

9. DDoS Attack (Distributed Denial-of-Service)

A DDoS attack involves overwhelming a target system, such as a website or server, with a massive amount of traffic, rendering it inaccessible to legitimate users.

10. Social Engineering

Social engineering is the manipulation of individuals into divulging confidential information by exploiting human psychology rather than using technical hacking methods.

11. Botnet

A botnet is a network of infected computers or devices controlled by a hacker, often used to carry out large-scale cyberattacks, such as DDoS attacks or data theft.

12. Vulnerability

A vulnerability refers to a weakness or flaw in a system or software that can be exploited by cybercriminals to gain unauthorized access or cause damage.

13. Antivirus Software

Antivirus software is designed to detect, prevent, and remove malware from computer systems, protecting against a wide range of cyber threats, including viruses, worms, and trojans.

14. Patch Management

Patch management is the process of identifying, testing, and applying software updates to fix vulnerabilities, enhance performance, and ensure security within an organization’s systems.

15. Endpoint Security

Endpoint security involves protecting individual devices, such as computers, smartphones, and tablets, from cyber threats, ensuring that each device is secure against potential vulnerabilities.

16. Intrusion Detection System (IDS)

An intrusion detection system is a security tool that monitors network traffic for suspicious activities, identifying and alerting administrators to potential intrusions or breaches.

17.Encryption Key

An encryption key is a string of characters used in encryption algorithms to encrypt or decrypt data, ensuring that sensitive information remains protected from unauthorized access.

18. Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)

An advanced persistent threat is a prolonged and targeted cyberattack in which hackers gain unauthorized access to a system and remain undetected for an extended period to steal sensitive data.

19. Privilege Escalation

Privilege escalation occurs when a user or attacker gains higher-level access or permissions within a system, often exploiting vulnerabilities to perform unauthorized actions or access restricted data.

20. Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)

SIEM is a security solution that collects and analyzes security data from various sources to detect, respond to, and prevent potential cybersecurity threats in real time.
