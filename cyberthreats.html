CYBERSECURITY THREATS

FINEX outlines common cybersecurity threats that individuals and organizations should be aware of to protect their digital assets.

1 Phishing Attacks

Fraudulent attempts to obtain sensitive information by disguising as a trustworthy entity in emails or websites.

2 Ransomware

Malicious software that locks or encrypts data and demands payment for its release.

3 Malware

Software designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a system or network.

4 Trojan Horses

Malicious programs that disguise themselves as legitimate software to gain unauthorized access to systems.

5 Spyware

Software that secretly monitors and collects information about a user's activities without their consent.

6 Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) Attacks

Intercepting communication between two parties to steal or manipulate information.

7 SQL Injection

Attacking web applications by inserting malicious SQL queries into input fields to manipulate databases.

8 Denial-of-Service (DoS) Attacks

Overloading a system or network to make it unavailable to legitimate users.

9 Distributed Denial-of-Service (DDoS) Attacks

Using multiple systems to flood a target with traffic, causing it to crash or become unavailable.

10 Zero-Day Exploits

Attacks that exploit vulnerabilities in software that are unknown to the software vendor and have no patch available.

11 Brute Force Attacks

Trying multiple passwords or encryption keys to break into a system or account.

12 Credential Stuffing

Using stolen username and password pairs from previous breaches to access other accounts.

13 Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)

Inserting malicious scripts into websites to be executed by users' browsers, often used for stealing session cookies.

14 Privilege Escalation

Gaining elevated access rights to systems or networks to perform unauthorized actions.

15 Social Engineering

Manipulating individuals into revealing confidential information by exploiting psychological tactics.

16 Insider Threats

Cybersecurity threats originating from individuals within the organization, such as employees or contractors.

17 Botnets

Networks of compromised devices that can be controlled remotely to launch attacks or spread malware.

18 Adware

Unwanted software that displays advertisements, often bundled with other software, and can compromise user privacy.

19 Keylogging

The act of secretly recording the keystrokes of a user to capture sensitive information like passwords and personal data.

20 DNS Spoofing

Manipulating the Domain Name System to redirect users to fraudulent websites or intercept communication.
